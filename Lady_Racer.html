Lady Racer

Life in the fast lane.

British Powerboat Champion, World Record holder and promoter in her sport. Businesswomen, BBC and Sky Sports presenter, motivational speaker and proud YB of Trinity House.

Shelley has been racing Powerboats for over 20 years now, forging her way through the male-dominated sport of powerboat racing to make a name for herself both on the water and in the press from day one and achieving the title as one of the top racers in the world.

For me, powerboat racing isn't just a sport; it's a passion that has defined a huge part of my life. It's about pushing limits, the roar of the engine, the spray of the sea, and the relentless pursuit of victory. From my first race to becoming a World Champion, every moment on the water has been a lesson in resilience, strategy, and courage.

The Comeback

In 2016, I returned to racing full time after a 5-year break from my career due to a serious racing accident that nearly ended my career and life. Winning my class and taking second overall in the Cowes Torquay Cowes Marathon race made me the highest-ranked British female finisher ever. The team, Blastoff Racing with throttleman Dorian Griffiths, then went on to take a World Record on Lake Coniston, where the year before I received the coveted K7 Gold Star for 114mph.
